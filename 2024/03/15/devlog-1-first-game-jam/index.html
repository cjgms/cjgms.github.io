Devlog #1 — How I Built My First Game Jam Entry

Created2024-03-15|Updated2026-03-30|Devlog
|Post Views:

The Brief

Ludum Dare 54’s theme was “Limited Space”. I had 48 hours and zero plan.

This is how I shipped a game anyway.

Hour 1–3: Panic and Pivoting

My first idea was a space-station management sim. Too complex. Second idea: a puzzle game where you fold space. Still too complex.

Final idea: a platformer where the level constantly shrinks. Simple enough to finish, weird enough to be interesting.

The Tech Stack

  • Engine: Unity 2022.3 LTS
  • Graphics: URP with a custom 2D lighting shader
  • Audio: FMOD for adaptive music
  • Art: Aseprite for pixel sprites

What Went Right

The shrinking mechanic worked on the very first prototype. That was a good sign. I spent the rest of the time on juice — screenshake, particle effects, satisfying sound design.

What Went Wrong

I underestimated the level design time. I planned 20 levels and shipped 8. Lesson learned: cut scope early, not late.

The Result

Project Alpha got rated 4.2/5 for innovation and 3.8/5 overall. Not bad for a first jam!
