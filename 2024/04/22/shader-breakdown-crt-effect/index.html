Why Shaders?

Post-processing shaders are one of my favourite ways to establish visual identity quickly. A well-crafted shader can transform a game from “looks fine” to “has a distinct aesthetic”.

For Neon Drift, I wanted that retro arcade monitor feeling. Here’s how I built it.

The Effect

The shader combines three techniques:

  1. Scanlines — horizontal dark bands mimicking CRT phosphor rows
  2. Chromatic Aberration — RGB channel separation for that “old lens” look
  3. Vignette — darkened edges to draw focus to the centre

Performance Considerations

This runs as a full-screen post-processing pass, so it’s applied once per frame regardless of scene complexity. On mobile, consider reducing _ScanlineIntensity to 0 to skip that pass entirely.
